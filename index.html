Design of Spiral Casing

ABSTRACT
Spiral casing is an important part of Francis turbine for even distribution of kinetic energy of water. In spiral casing angular momentum of fluid entering into the guide vane and runner is constant. Stay ring is responsible for mechanical strain of spiral casing and it provides guidance to the water through stay vanes. In this project, the design of spiral casing has been done with the given value of Head, Flow rate and Rotational speed. The area of flow of spiral casing is decreased uniformly in order to keep velocity constant. Among the many design approaches for spiral casing F.de Siervo and F.de Leva Method is used as basis for our design. The design is based on the potential axis symmetric flow assumption. This design is done using SOLIDWORKS while simulation and analysis of design is done using ANSYS. The assumption for the conservation of the angular momentum is verified using CFD.

Steady flow past a cylinder at
Low Reynold's number

When steady flow at very low Reynold's number passes through a cylinder, we observe drop in pressure as well as velocity as flow past the cylinder. Viscous effect is very significant in this region and thus, Bernoulli equation is not satisfied. Overall loss of energy is manifested by low pressure behind the cylinder, which results in net pressure force known as drag.

Effect of changing geometric parameters in
Venturimeter

Venturimeter is an obstruction type flowmeter which is used to measure flowrate in pipes. Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) technique is used to analyze effect of different geometrical parameters on pressure drop in venturimeter. It is observed that at constant convergent cone angle, the pressure drop at first increases with increase in divergent cone angle, becomes maximum and then decreases sharply.
